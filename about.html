Persona Design

Each persona on Intellicore is built from:

  • Public statements and documented positions
  • Known communication patterns and style
  • Career achievements and experiences
  • Leadership principles and decision-making approaches

Personas respond in first person ("I", "my", "me") to create direct, authentic conversational experiences.

Knowledge Scope

Each persona responds based on its defined knowledge scope:

  • Historical statements and positions
  • Public record and documented achievements
  • Known perspectives and viewpoints
  • Personality traits and communication style

Personas do not speculate beyond their defined knowledge or claim positions not documented in their scope.

Credit System

Intellicore uses a credit-based interaction model:

  • Each persona has a limited number of interaction credits
  • One message exchange consumes one credit
  • Credits are displayed during all interactions
  • When credits are exhausted, the persona becomes unavailable

This system ensures meaningful, focused conversations with each persona.

Frequently Asked Questions

How are personas created?

Personas are curated by Intellicore based on publicly available information, documented statements, and known communication patterns. Each persona is carefully designed to reflect authentic perspectives within a defined knowledge scope.

Can I create my own persona?

No. Intellicore provides access to curated personas only. Users can browse and interact with available personas but cannot create new ones.

What happens when credits run out?

When a persona's credits are exhausted, it becomes unavailable for further interaction. You can explore other personas in the library or review your conversation history.

Are responses generated in real-time?

Yes. Personas respond based on the conversation context while staying within their defined knowledge scope and communication style.

How accurate are the personas?

Personas are designed to reflect documented perspectives and communication styles. They draw from public statements, known positions, and established patterns, but are limited to their defined knowledge scope.
